# [\#9119 Issue](https://github.com/ethereum/solidity/issues/9119) `closed`: Optimize AND/SHL
**Labels**: `optimizer`, `low effort`, `medium impact`, `should have`


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/9073706?v=4" width="50">[chriseth](https://github.com/chriseth) opened issue at [2020-06-04 08:46](https://github.com/ethereum/solidity/issues/9119):

``and(shl(A, X), shl(A, Y))``
should be optimized to
``shl(A, and(X, Y))``

or even

``and(shl(Z, X), shl(Z, Y))``
should be optimized to
``shl(Z, and(X, Y))``


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/702124?u=00e20e1963ccc9a908a5826b2d8c3b1b1f6acea4&v=4" width="50">[k06a](https://github.com/k06a) commented at [2022-02-02 15:24](https://github.com/ethereum/solidity/issues/9119#issuecomment-1028052908):

@chriseth I can't find any difference between the 2 provided examples? Is it a typo?

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/1347491?v=4" width="50">[ekpyron](https://github.com/ekpyron) commented at [2022-02-02 15:31](https://github.com/ethereum/solidity/issues/9119#issuecomment-1028060749):

The difference stems from how we internally formulate simplification rules in https://github.com/ethereum/solidity/blob/develop/libevmasm/RuleList.h and isn't clear in the issue description - we use ``A, B, C, ...`` for constants and ``X, Y, Z, ...`` for arbitrary expressions when pattern matching simplification rules.
